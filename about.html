Katie M. Flynn is a writer, editor, and educator living in San Francisco. Her writing has appeared in many journals, most recently Carve, Hobart, Monkeybicycle, and Superstition Review. She’s been nominated for a Pushcart Prize twice and holds an MFA from the University of San Francisco. For the past ten years she has taught writing and run a writing center. Now, she serves as Fiction Editor at The Indianola Review.
Before earning her MFA, Katie studied geography and border politics at UCLA and worked as a cartographer. When she’s not writing, she’s teaching herself classical guitar, nerding out over chess, or chasing her two kids through the wilds of San Francisco.
